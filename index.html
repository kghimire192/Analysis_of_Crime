Introduction

Public safety is one of the most fundamental rights of people. To understand the level of public safety in an area, it is important to look at their crime rates. Identifying regions with high crime rates allows for further investigation of underlying reasons for crimes, and it also allows policymakers to deal with high (or increased) crime rates in certain region.


Overview

The analysis process of the project is to understand crime rates in the United States across time and across geographical regions. The years used in the analysis is from year 2010 to 2017. District of Columbia has been treated as a US state in the analsis. Similarly, the geographical regions used in the analysis include New England, Mid-Atlantic, East North Central, West North Central, South Atlantic, East South Central, West South Central, Mountain and Pacific. Violent crimes and property crimes are two groups of crimes used in analysis. The crime data came from the annual Uniform Crime Reports by the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) on FBIâ€™s official website.

Total Crime Rate by Region

Correlation between Violent and Property Crime Rates

The bubble chart below captures the relationship between violent crime rates and property crime rates. The average crime rates (from 2010 to 2017) has been used to plot the chart. The graph shows that the region with high violent crime rate seemed to also have high property crime rate. Similarly, the regions with low property crime rate also seemed to have lower violent crime rate.
