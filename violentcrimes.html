Violent Crimes

The violent crimes used in the analysis include murder and manslaughter, rape, robbery and aggravated assault.

The pie chart below shows the breakdown of the combined violent crimes in the United States from 2010 to 2017. The aggravated assault was identified as the top one with 62.1% of the total violent crimes, whereas murder and manslaughter accounted for only 1.3% of the total violent crimes.

Violent Crime Rate by State

The bar graph below shows the violent crime rate (per 100,000 population) for each state. The bar graphs identifies District of Columbia as the state the highest violent crime rates for each year from 2010 to 2017. Similarly, Maine and Vermont were identified as the two states with the lowest violent crime rates. Different years can be selected from the dropdown menu.



Violent Crime Growth Rate

The Geo chart below shows the violent crime rate growth from 2010 to 2017 for each of the US states. The figure identifies South Dakota as the state with highest increase (72.51%) in violent crime rate from 2010 to 2017. Similarly, New Jersey had the highest drop (23.84%) in violent crime rates.



Violent Crime Rate by Region

The line plot below acts as a time-series plot for violent crime rate for each region from 2010 to 2017. The overall trend seems to be different for each region. West South Central had the highest violent crime rate in most of the years from 2010 to 2017. It dropped its crime rate by 2013 and then again increased by 2017. Similarly, New England had the second lowest violent crime rate in 2010 and the lowest in 2017.
